Summary

Conclusions & Recommendations

Key conclusions derived from the findings and actionable recommendations for the University of La Salette, Inc.

📋

Moderate Help-Seeking

Students exhibit moderate help-seeking behavior (M=3.20). They are more likely to seek help for academic concerns but tend to be self-reliant in handling personal issues.

🏛️

Utilization Gap

POD and OSSA are frequently utilized ("Often"), while Faculty Support, HDC, and DSS remain only moderately used. A gap exists between awareness and actual service use.

⚖️

Sex & Year Level

No significant differences in help-seeking behavior or support utilization based on sex or year level, suggesting equitable access and engagement patterns.

🎓

Department Matters

Significant differences across departments in HSB, HDC, DSS, POD, and OSSA (p < .05). Academic environment and departmental culture influence engagement patterns.

🔗

Positive Relationship

A significant positive relationship exists between help-seeking behavior and all support systems. Students who seek help more are more likely to utilize institutional services.

💡

Recommendations

Strengthen proactive help-seeking initiatives, enhance visibility of underutilized services (FS, HDC, DSS), implement department-based interventions, and improve integration among all support systems.
