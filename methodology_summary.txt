RESEARCH METHODOLOGY SUMMARY

PROJECT:   Help-Seeking Behavior & Support Systems (ULS, Inc.)
LOCALE:    University of La Salette, Inc., Santiago City
TIMEFRAME: AY 2025-2026, 2nd Semester

1. RESEARCH DESIGN & LOCALE
   Design Type : Quantitative Descriptive-Correlational
   Study Site  : University of La Salette, Inc. (ULS) main campus

2. PARTICIPANTS & SAMPLING
   Population (N) : 5,821 undergraduate students
   Sample Size (n) : 361 respondents (determined via Raosoft calculator)
   Sampling Method : Proportional Stratified Random Sampling
   Year Level Distribution:
     Freshmen   : 102 respondents (28.3%)
     Sophomores :  99 respondents (27.4%)
     Juniors    : 160 respondents (44.3%)
   Note: Seniors were excluded due to off-campus internship/clinical schedules.

3. RESEARCH INSTRUMENT
   Structure: Two-part Survey Questionnaire
     Part I  : Demographic Profile (Age, Sex, Year Level, Department)
     Part II : Help-Seeking Behavior & 5 support units (Faculty, HDC, DSS, POD, OSSA)
   Measurement Scale: 5-Point Likert Scale (1-Never, 2-Rarely, 3-Sometimes, 4-Often, 5-Always)
   Reliability Index: Pilot testing Cronbach's Alpha = 0.953 (Excellent consistency)

4. OPERATIONAL FLOW
   Approval ──> Validation ──> Pilot Test ──> Survey ──> Data Encoding

   Approval      : Institutional permissions secured from President & College Deans.
   Validation    : Vetted by academic experts for relevance, clarity, and formatting.
   Pilot Test    : Pre-tested on a small representative cohort to verify reliability.
   Survey        : Personally distributed face-to-face; retrieved within 3–5 minutes.
   Data Encoding : Checked for completion and compiled into Microsoft Excel.

5. ETHICAL PROTOCOLS
   Core Pillars : Beneficence, Non-Maleficence, Justice, Respect for Persons.
   Authorization: Reviewed and approved by the Research Adviser prior to gathering.
   Privacy      : Voluntary participation, optional anonymity, & encrypted database.

6. STATISTICAL TREATMENT
   Normality Vetting : Shapiro-Wilk Test (p < 0.05) ──> Confirmed non-normal dataset.
   Statistical Path  : Nonparametric tests utilized for inferential analysis.
   Applied Tools     :
     Demographic Profile  : Frequency Counts & Percentages
     Behavior/Usage Levels: Mean & Standard Deviation (SD)
     Gender Differences   : Mann-Whitney U Test
     Year/Dept Differences: Kruskal-Wallis H Test
     Core Relationship    : Spearman’s rho correlation coefficient (sig. at 0.05)
